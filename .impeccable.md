# Rizqak Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Job seekers in Egypt and Africa looking for verified employment opportunities in Gulf countries and the Arab world. They use Rizqak primarily on mobile devices, often in moments of hope and urgency — browsing during commutes, evenings, or between tasks. They need to quickly assess job legitimacy, compare opportunities, and apply with confidence. Employers and recruiters are secondary users who post and manage listings.

### Brand Personality
**Professional & Trustworthy** · **Warm & Approachable** · **Calm & Reliable**
Voice is supportive and authoritative — like a knowledgeable mentor who genuinely cares. Tone is clear, respectful, and never hype-driven. The platform should feel like a safe, well-organized space where every opportunity has been vetted. Emotional goals: confidence in applying, hope for career growth, clarity in the process, and just enough urgency to act.

### Aesthetic Direction
Clean and structured with a green-and-gold palette that conveys growth and value. Zero border-radius throughout (sharp, rectangular corners) gives a distinctive, modern edge. Cairo typeface bridges Arabic-first and Latin content beautifully. Dark mode is fully supported. The visual language is mature — generous whitespace, clear hierarchy, subtle shadows, purposeful animations. Not playful or whimsical; not cold or corporate. Reference feel: think LinkedIn's trustworthiness meets a well-crafted Arabic-first product.

**Anti-references**: No rounded bubbly UI, no excessive gradients, no gamification elements, no neon/oversaturated colors.

### Design Principles

1. **Trust First** — Every design decision should reinforce legitimacy. Verified badges, clear company info, transparent job details. Users must never question whether an opportunity is real.

2. **Clarity Over Decoration** — Information hierarchy must be immediately obvious. Prioritize scannable layouts, clear labels, and progressive disclosure over visual flourishes.

3. **RTL-Native, Not RTL-Adjusted** — The Arabic experience is the primary design, not a mirrored version of English. Use logical properties (`inset-inline-start`, `margin-inline`, etc.) throughout. Typography, spacing, and layout must feel natural in both directions.

4. **Accessible by Default** — WCAG 2.1 AAA target. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Every interactive element must be keyboard-navigable with visible focus indicators. Color is never the sole means of conveying information.

5. **Performance Is UX** — Job seekers on slower connections cannot wait. Lazy-load aggressively, keep bundles lean, skeleton states for every async boundary. The interface must feel instant.

## Technical Context

### Stack
- **Framework**: Nuxt 4 (Vue 3, SPA mode)
- **Styling**: Tailwind CSS 4 with comprehensive custom design tokens
- **Components**: Reka UI (headless), CVA + tailwind-merge for variants
- **State**: Pinia, TanStack Vue Query
- **Forms**: VeeValidate + Zod
- **i18n**: @nuxtjs/i18n — Arabic (default, RTL) + English (LTR)
- **Icons**: lucide-vue-next
- **PWA**: @vite-pwa/nuxt (standalone display)

### Design Tokens (Key Values)
- **Primary green**: `#047857` (base), `#1B6B4A` (theme color)
- **Gold accent**: `#cf9e1c`
- **Font**: Cairo (300–800 weights)
- **Border radius**: All `0` (brand standard — sharp corners)
- **Container max**: `1200px` (shell: `80rem`)
- **Shell header height**: `56px`
- **Spacing scale**: compact=8px, element=12px, content=16px, section=32px

### Conventions
- Semantic tokens prefixed `--color-*`, `--spacing-*`, `--font-size-ds-*`
- Shell components use `shell-*` CSS classes (header, nav, buttons)
- Base components: `BaseTypography`, `BaseButton` (in `shared/components/base/`)
- Logical properties for RTL support (`inset-inline-*`, `margin-inline-*`)
- Class-variance-authority for component variants
- Tailwind merge for class composition
